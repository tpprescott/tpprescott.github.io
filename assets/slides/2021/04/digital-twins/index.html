Layered network decomposition

Signal propagation

The influence of $k_a$ on $X_4$?

Layers in isolation and in context

  • System dynamics: \[ \dot x = f(x) = f_1(x) + f_2(x) \]
  • Feedback interconnection of layers introduced by replicating state vector: \begin{align} \dot x_1 &= f_1(x_1+x_2) \\ \dot x_2 &= f_2(x_1+x_2) \end{align}

Example: Biochemical reaction networks

  • Decomposition into groups of reactions: \[ \dot x = \left[ \begin{matrix} S_1 & S_2 \end{matrix} \right] \left[ \begin{matrix} v_1(x) \\ v_2(x) \end{matrix} \right] \]
  • Feedback interconnection of layers: \begin{align} \dot x_1 &= S_1 v_1(x_1+x_2) \\ \dot x_2 &= S_2 v_2(x_1+x_2) \end{align}

  • Approach is orthogonal to typical modular decompositions.
  • States are untransformed and thus intepretable.
  • Signal propagation is explicit (retroactivity).
  • Parametric perturbations propagate across layers in feedback.

Error guarantees

Tools from robust control theory

Emphasis on input/output guarantees

Exact and approximate models: \begin{align} \dot x &= f(x,u) &y = h(x,u) \\ \dot{\tilde{x}} &= \tilde f(\tilde x, u) &{\tilde y} = \tilde h(\tilde x, u) \end{align}

Error system:

\begin{align} \frac{d}{dt} \left[ \begin{matrix} x \\ \tilde x \end{matrix} \right] &= F(x, \tilde x, u) & y_e = h(x, u) - \tilde h(\tilde x, u) \end{align}

\begin{align} \frac{d}{dt} \left[ \begin{matrix} x \\ \tilde x \end{matrix} \right] &= F(x, \tilde x, u) & y_e = h(x, u) - \tilde h(\tilde x, u) \end{align}
Can use dissipativity to certify error-system properties; e.g. the error bound \[ \| y_e \|_2^2 \leq \gamma^2 \| u \|_2^2 + V(x_0, \tilde x_0) \]
is certified by finding $V(x, \tilde x) > 0$ and $\gamma$ s.t. \[ \nabla V(x, \tilde x) \cdot F(x, \tilde x, u) + |h(x,u)-\tilde h(\tilde x, u)|^2 \leq \gamma^2 |u|^2 \]

Multifidelity likelihood-free inference

Likelihood-free Bayesian parameter inference

  • Explicit modelling of complex, stochastic, parametrised dynamical systems mapping parameters to outputs.
  • Can simulate $y \sim f(\cdot~|~\theta)$ but cannot calculate $L(\theta) = f(\mathrm{data}~|~\theta)$.

ABC

For neighbourhood of data, $\Omega$, estimate the likelihood: \[ L_{\mathrm{ABC}}(\theta) = \mathbf 1(y \in \Omega) \] Simulation $y \sim f(\cdot~|~\theta)$ is a bottleneck - to what extent can we use a different, cheaper model?

We consider a cheaper surrogate, $\tilde y \sim \tilde f(\cdot~|~\theta)$ and construct: \[ L_{\mathrm{mf}}(\theta) = \mathbf 1(\tilde y \in \Omega) + \frac{\mathbf 1(U \leq \alpha)}{\alpha} \left[ \mathbf 1(y \in \Omega) - \mathbf 1(\tilde y \in \Omega) \right] \] With probability $1-\alpha$ we do not simulate $y \sim f(\cdot~|~\theta)$.

Agent-based stochastic models of cell motility

  • Parametrised SDE.
  • Cell velocity evolves as a random walk on parametrised stationary distributions.
  • Electric field induces a new stationary distribution.

Quantifying the impact of electric fields on single-cell motility

Paper uses likelihood-free Bayesian inference to identify model parameters from data.

We thus identify way(s) in which electric fields lead to observed biases in cell motility.
